Why I decided to be a teacher

I didn't decide to be a teacher until about term 1 of year 12 in 2020, For a long time I was throwing around lots of ideas of what I could potentially do, go into finance, be a tradie etc, but I've always thought that I couldn't handle an office job because I just thought it'd be boring, which made me think of entering the trades but then I realised that not only could I not do the early mornings, but I don't think my body could cope with the job itself. Thats when it hit me, I could do teaching to inspire the next few generations to keep carpentry going as an important life skill, as well as a hobby. My main inspiration to become a teacher was my Year 12 timber teacher, who really helped me produce an amazing majorwork, which made me want to do the same for others.

My Teaching Philosophy

As a 21-year-old woodwork teacher, I believe that teaching is about more than just imparting technical skillsâ€”it's about inspiring creativity, fostering a love for craftsmanship, and encouraging a mindset of curiosity and resilience. I see myself not just as an instructor but as a mentor who guides students in discovering their potential through hands-on learning and exploration.

In my woodwork classes, I aim to create an environment that is both safe and inspiring. I understand that working with tools and materials can be intimidating for some, so I focus on building a foundation of safety and confidence. By teaching my students how to properly use and respect the tools of the trade, I help them develop a sense of responsibility and pride in their work.

I am committed to fostering an inclusive classroom where every student feels valued and supported. I believe that diversity in thought, experience, and perspective enriches the learning environment, and I strive to create a space where all students can express their ideas and creativity. I encourage collaboration and peer learning, understanding that we all have something to learn from one another.

My teaching philosophy centres on the importance of process over product. While I aim to help my students create beautiful and functional pieces, I also want them to appreciate the journey of learning, problem-solving, and personal growth that occurs along the way. I believe that mistakes are valuable learning opportunities, and I encourage my students to take risks, experiment, and learn from their experiences.

As a woodwork teacher, I am passionate about the power of craftsmanship to teach patience, attention to detail, and perseverance. I believe that through working with their hands, students can develop a deeper understanding of themselves and the world around them. My goal is to help them cultivate a lifelong love for creating and to empower them to apply the skills and values they learn in the workshop to other areas of their lives.

Ultimately, I see teaching as a way to inspire and nurture the next generation of makers and thinkers. I am dedicated to helping my students not only develop their technical skills but also grow as confident, creative, and thoughtful individuals.
